About me

I am currently a Postdoc at INRIA Sophia Antipolis. I am working with Dr. K. Avrachenkov in group NEO. I reveived my PhD (2015-2019) from the Department of Telecommunication and Information Processing (TELIN) at Ghent University. My dissertation is titled "Stochastic Modelling of Wireless sensor Networks" and was carried under the supervision of Prof. Dieter Fiems and Dr. Koen De Turck. Prior to that, I obtained my masters degree in Industrial engineering and opreations research (IEOR) (2012-2014) from the Indian institute of Technology (IIT) Bombay.

My research interests broadly lie in Stochastic approximations, optimal control, Reinforcement learning, Queueing systems, Stochastic modelling, Markov decision process (MDP) and Game Theory. In large scale WSNs, my recent focus is on developing the analytical model using approximation techniques such as heavy-traffic limits, fluid and diffusion limits, and mean field approximations. For more details, feel free to browse my Publications page.
